# Noise and Processing Noise in Auditory Brainstem Responses


## An Overview of the Problem

Our research is aimed to develop a **faster** method of using Auditory Brainstem Response **determining the hearing threshold** for individuals that cannot make a observables response upon instructions, like **infants** and animals.

### Why is this Important

According to a 2019 CDC statistics, 3 in 1000 babies have hearing loss. However, across United States, there are only 1.7 out of 1000 babies are screened for hearing loss when they are born. The reason of such a low screening rate is the current screening method takes a long time to get an accurate result and requires baby to be in a calm sleep. As we could develop a faster diagnostic method, we could screen every single new-born, allowing them to be treated as early as possible.

### What is Auditory Brainstem Response(ABR)

[Auditory Brainstem Response(ABR)](https://www.asha.org/public/hearing/auditory-brainstem-response/) is a test that used with children or others who cannot complete a typical hearing screening. It tells us how the inner ear, called the cochlea, and the brain pathways for hearing are working.
